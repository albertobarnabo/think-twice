# Code of Conduct

lazy-cat is built on one principle: do what's needed, nothing more. That applies to code and to how we treat each other here.

## The short version

Be direct. Be kind. Focus on the work.

## What we expect

- Critique the code, not the person
- Be honest — including about your own mistakes
- Give feedback that's specific and actionable, not just critical
- Make room for contributors at every experience level
- If something isn't working, say so clearly and move on

## What we don't tolerate

- Harassment, discrimination, or personal attacks of any kind
- Dismissing a contribution without explanation
- Bad faith engagement — arguing to win rather than to improve the project

## Enforcement

Violations can be reported by opening a private issue or emailing the maintainers directly via GitHub. We will respond within 5 working days. Maintainers reserve the right to remove comments, close issues, or ban contributors who repeatedly violate this policy.
